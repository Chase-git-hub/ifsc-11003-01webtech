University of Arkansas at Little Rock: A Brief History

History

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) is a public research university in Little Rock, Arkansas. Established as Little Rock Junior College by the Little Rock School District in 1927, the institution became a private four-year university under the name Little Rock University in 1957. It returned to public status in 1969 when it merged with the University of Arkansas System under its present name.

Campus

At 250 acres (100 ha), the UA Little Rock campus encompasses more than 56 buildings, including the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Sciences, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and the Ottenheimer Library. Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities such as art galleries, a public radio station, and a campus-wide wireless network. It is classified as an R2: High Research Activity institution.

Sports

UA Little Rock's athletic teams are known as the Little Rock Trojans, with almost all teams competing in the Sun Belt Conference. Wrestling became the newest sport in 2019 and is the first Division I program of its kind in Arkansas.
