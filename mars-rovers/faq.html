Common Questions

How do the Mars rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers send data through orbiters circling Mars, which then transmit signals back to Earth using NASAâ€™s Deep Space Network.

Why does NASA send rovers instead of humans?

Rovers are safer and more cost-effective for early exploration. They gather data crucial for preparing future human missions.

What powers the rovers?

Curiosity and Perseverance are powered by RTGs, which convert heat from decaying plutonium into electricity.

Can the rovers return to Earth?

No. They are designed to operate on Mars permanently, though Perseverance is collecting samples for a future return mission.
