Common Questions

How do the Mars rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers send data through Mars orbiters, which relay signals back to NASAâ€™s Deep Space Network antennas on Earth.

Why send rovers instead of humans?

Rovers provide a safer, cost-effective way to study Mars, gathering vital data for planning future crewed missions.

What powers the rovers?

Curiosity and Perseverance use RTGs that convert heat from decaying plutonium into electricity for long-term missions.

Can rovers return to Earth?

No. They're designed to operate permanently on Mars. Perseverance, however, is caching samples for a future return mission.
