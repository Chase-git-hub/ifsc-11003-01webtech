Exploring the Red Planet

The NASA Mars Rover program represents humanity's bold effort to explore the Martian surface. Each rover has advanced our understanding of Mars, searching for signs of water, studying geology, and testing new technologies that will pave the way for future human missions.

Featured Rovers

Curiosity Rover

  • Mission goals: Assess past habitability; study geology and climate.
  • Launch/Landing: Nov 26, 2011 / Aug 6, 2012 (Gale Crater).
  • Discoveries: Ancient lake environments; organics; methane variations.
  • Key features: RTG power source, ChemCam laser, SAM analysis suite.

Perseverance Rover

  • Mission goals: Seek biosignatures; cache rock samples for return.
  • Launch/Landing: Jul 30, 2020 / Feb 18, 2021 (Jezero Crater).
  • Discoveries: River delta deposits; igneous rocks; preserved organic carbon.
  • Key features: Sample caching arm, Ingenuity helicopter, SHERLOC & PIXL tools.
