Exploring the Red Planet

The NASA Mars Rover program represents humanity’s bold effort to explore the Martian surface. Each rover has advanced our understanding of Mars, searching for signs of water, studying geology, and testing new technologies that will pave the way for future human missions.

Featured Rovers

Curiosity Rover

Launched in 2011, Curiosity’s mission was to determine whether Mars ever had the right conditions to support life. It landed in Gale Crater in 2012 and continues to investigate the planet’s geology and climate.

Perseverance Rover

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater in 2021, carrying the first helicopter ever to fly on another planet — Ingenuity. Its mission includes collecting rock samples for future return to Earth and studying the potential for ancient microbial life.
